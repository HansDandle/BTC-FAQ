## Senate Banking Committee Markup: The Regulatory Turning Point

The US Senate Banking Committee is scheduled to vote on the **Digital Asset Market Clarity Act (CLARITY)** on January 15, marking what could be a watershed moment for cryptocurrency regulation[1]. This markup represents the first serious attempt at comprehensive federal crypto legislation, addressing a regulatory vacuum that has plagued the industry for years. If approved by the committee, the bill would advance to the full Senate for consideration, potentially establishing clear rules for exchanges, brokers, and dealers similar to traditional financial markets[1].

The bill's core innovation is the **"maturity test,"** which assesses whether a digital asset remains under centralized control or has achieved sufficient decentralization to transition from securities classification to commodities[1]. This framework could provide crucial clarity for thousands of altcoins currently trapped in regulatory limbo. The CFTC would oversee digital commodities like Bitcoin, while the SEC retains authority over assets deemed securities[1].

## Stablecoin Debates and DeFi Friction Points

Negotiations among senators continue on two contentious issues: how to regulate decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols and whether crypto platforms should offer yield on stablecoin deposits[1]. Senator Angela Alsobrooks (D-MD) has proposed allowing exchanges to pay rewards on transactions with dollar-pegged stablecoins while barring rewards on tokens held in digital wallets[2]. These distinctions reflect broader concerns about stablecoin adoption's impact on traditional banking and financial stability.

Meanwhile, the Senate Agriculture Committee has encountered bipartisan friction, with Democratic Senator Cory Booker reportedly withholding support from Chairman John Boozman's draft[2]. This disagreement could jeopardize the unified approach needed for passage. The Bank Policy Institute has raised concerns about unregulated crypto risks, including stablecoin interest payments and DeFi lending impacts[2].

## Institutional Adoption Accelerates Amid Regulatory Progress

Beyond the Senate, institutional adoption is accelerating as regulatory frameworks mature globally[3]. The GENIUS Act, enacted in late 2025, has already prompted the FDIC to propose licensing rules for banks issuing stablecoins through subsidiaries[2]. World Liberty Financial, a Trump-affiliated crypto firm, has applied for an OCC national trust bank charter to issue its USD1 stablecoin[2]. Wyoming has also issued the first state-backed stablecoin, backed by a U.S. state[2].

Regulators worldwide are implementing sandbox initiatives and reducing licensing friction to foster innovation[3]. This trend signals that major financial institutions are becoming increasingly comfortable with digital assets and stablecoin infrastructure[3].
